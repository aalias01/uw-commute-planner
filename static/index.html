Built around one question: when should I leave, and from where, to catch the best connection home?

Why it exists

General transit apps are great at showing routes, but they rarely answer a narrow, repeated decision well. This app is built around one real commute from UW. Instead of searching broadly, it works backwards from the destination and finds the best live option right now.

How the planner decides

It solves the commute as a connected chain. Starting from your final bus home, it finds the right train at U-District Station, then checks which feeder bus from campus (or a direct walk) gets you to that train on time. Every viable chain is scored: least wait at the final transfer station wins, then least extra wait along the way, then the earliest departure when those are close.

Leaving sooner isn't always better. Most transit apps optimize for departure time, not transfer wait. This one picks the trip where you spend the least time waiting at connections.
What's in each tab
  • Planner: live ranked recommendations with transfer buffer, reliability labels, and fallback options when no trips fit your window.
  • Active: follow a chosen plan and refresh to re-query live predictions for the same train and bus trips while you travel.
  • Timetable: live departure boards for feeder buses, Link light rail, final buses, and class-bound routes.
Tech stack
  • Backend: Python, FastAPI, httpx, uvicorn
  • Frontend: Vanilla HTML / CSS / JavaScript
  • Data: OneBusAway real-time transit API
  • Hosting: Vercel
